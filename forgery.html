Dedicated to the study of

Iran Postal History

It is with deep regret that I must acknowledge the prevalence of forgeries in Iranian philately. This abundance of counterfeit items poses significant challenges for collectors, wasting their valuable resources—both time and money—and leading to the disheartening realization that their cherished collections may also be tainted by these forgeries. This issue has driven many enthusiasts to abandon their pursuit of philately altogether.
Exacerbating the problem is the lack of accessible resources to educate collectors about these forgeries. Historically, some experts have been reluctant to share their knowledge publicly, fearing that such information would aid forgers in improving their techniques, thus worsening the problem. I fundamentally oppose this viewpoint. I believe that the most effective way to combat the plague of forgery is to equip every collector with as much knowledge as possible to identify counterfeits. This is one of the primary objectives of this website. On this page, I will share examples of forgeries I have encountered, enabling everyone to recognize these counterfeits.
A couple of very important notes:
1- Detecting forgeries is an evolving science, as forgers continually develop new methods to make their products resemble genuine items. Therefore, the information shared here is not a complete and foolproof guide but a simply a mere starting point.
2- The safest and most reliable way to verify the authenticity of an item is to seek a certificate from an expert. While experts may occasionally make mistakes, they possess the best tools and techniques to distinguish genuine items from forgeries. For Iranian items, Mr Sadri and Iran Philatelic Study Circle (IPSC) issue trustworthy certificates.
